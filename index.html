Tribute to Alan Turing

Alan Turing was a pioneering computer scientist, mathematician, logician, cryptanalyst, philosopher, and theoretical biologist. He made major contributions to mathematics and the development of computer science.

Timeline

1912

Born in London, England.

1936

Published a paper on the Turing Machine, laying the groundwork for computer science.

1939-1945

Worked at Bletchley Park to break the Enigma code during World War II.

1950

Published "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" proposing the Turing Test.

1954

Passed away in Wilmslow, Cheshire, England.

Quotes

"We can only see a short distance ahead, but we can see plenty there that needs to be done."
"Those who can imagine anything, can create the impossible."
